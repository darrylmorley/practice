Linus Torvalds

The Creator of Linux

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1981 - Linus took an interest in computers, initially learning to program in BASIC on a Commodore VIC-20.
  • 1988 - Attended the University of Helsinki, leaving mid term to complete mandatory military service.
  • 1989 - Completed 11 months officer training to fulfill mandatory military service. He held the rank of 2nd Lieutenant.
  • 1990 - Linus returned to the University if Helsinki to complete his studies.
  • 1991 - Released the first prototype of Linux.
  • 1994 - Linux Version 1.0 was released.
  • 1996 - The asteroid 9793 Torvalds was named after him.
  • 1999 - Linus was named as one of the worlds top 100 innovators by MIT Technology Review.

Linus Torvalds is known for disagreeing with other developers on the Linux kernel mailing list. Calling himself a "really unpleasant person", he later explained "I’d like to be a nice person and curse less and encourage people to grow rather than telling them they are idiots. I’m sorry – I tried, it’s just not in me."
